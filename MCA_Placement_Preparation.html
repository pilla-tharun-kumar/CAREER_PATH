MONTHS 1–2 Quantitative Aptitude & Logical Reasoning Weeks 1–8
🎯 Goal: Score 80%+ in aptitude sections. Practice minimum 30 questions daily. Maintain a formula sheet in Notion.
CONCEPTS TO LEARN
Number Types: Natural, Whole, Integer, Rational, Irrational, Prime, Composite — definitions + examples
Divisibility Rules (2 to 13) — memorize all and practice with 3-digit numbers
HCF & LCM: Prime factorization method + shortcut formula (HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers)
Simplification (BODMAS) — practice 20 questions; focus on fractions and square roots
→ Practice
DAILY PRACTICE TARGETS
Mon–Fri: 30 questions/day from IndiaBIX — Number System section
→ Start Here
Sat–Sun: Take 1 full timed mock (60 min, 50 questions) on PrepInsta
→ Mock Tests
Add all new formulas to Notion formula sheet — review this sheet every morning for 10 minutes
CONCEPTS TO MASTER
Percentage conversions — memorize fraction-to-percent table (1/8 = 12.5%, 1/6 = 16.67%, etc.) — saves huge time
Profit & Loss formulas: CP, SP, Profit%, Loss%, Marked Price, Discount, Successive discount
Shortcut tricks for compound percentages (e.g. +20% then -20% = net -4%, not 0)
PRACTICE
30 questions/day: Percentages on Mon–Tue, Profit & Loss on Wed–Thu, Mixed on Fri
→ Practice
Weekend mock test — set a 45-minute timer, attempt 40 Quant questions, analyze errors
CONCEPTS
Time & Work: Efficiency method (if A does in N days, 1 day work = 1/N) — core shortcut for all variations
MDH (Man-Day-Hour) formula — M1D1H1/W1 = M2D2H2/W2 for extended problems
Pipes & Cisterns: Inlet/outlet concepts, combined rate formula
Wages & proportional distribution in work problems (company favourite!)
PRACTICE
30 questions/day from IndiaBIX Time & Work section
→ Practice
Solve 10 Pipes & Cistern questions as bonus — they're the same concept
→ Practice
CONCEPTS
Speed = Distance / Time — unit conversions (km/hr to m/s: multiply by 5/18)
Relative speed: same direction (subtract), opposite direction (add) — applied to trains and boats
Average Speed formula — 2ab/(a+b) when same distance at different speeds
Boats & Streams: Upstream = Speed(boat) - Speed(stream), Downstream = Speed(boat) + Speed(stream)
END-OF-MONTH-1 CHECK
Take a 100-question Quant mock test (timed: 70 minutes) — target score: 75+
→ Mock Test
Review all weak topics — make a list, dedicate extra 30 min/day in Month 2 to those
CONCEPTS
Ratio & Proportion basics: Compounded ratio, Duplicate ratio, Inverse ratio — all types with examples
Alligation Rule — cross-multiplication trick for mixture problems; practice 10 examples
Partnership problems — profit split based on time × investment
PRACTICE
30 questions/day from IndiaBIX — Ratio, Alligation sections
→ Practice
CONCEPTS
Fundamental Counting Principle, nPr, nCr — formula + Pascal's Triangle for combinations
Arrangements: with repetition, without repetition, circular arrangements
Probability basics: Classical, Conditional, Complementary, Independent events
Card, Dice, Coin problems — these 3 sample spaces appear in 90% of placement Probability questions
PRACTICE
Solve company-tagged questions on PrepInsta — TCS & Infosys love P&C problems
→ TCS Papers
CONCEPTS
Number Series & Alphabet Series: difference patterns, square/cube series, combined patterns
Coding-Decoding: Letter shift, symbol coding, word-to-number coding — spot the pattern systematically
Blood Relations: Draw family trees for every problem — do not solve in your head
Direction Sense: Always draw a compass before solving — North, South, East, West
PRACTICE
30 Logical Reasoning questions/day from IndiaBIX
→ Practice
CONCEPTS
Seating Arrangement: Linear (one row, two rows) and Circular — always make a diagram on paper
Syllogisms: Venn diagram method — faster than Aristotle's method for placements
Data Interpretation: Bar graph, Pie chart, Line graph, Table — practice reading data quickly without calculator
Clocks & Calendars — formula for angle between hands, odd days method for day of week

MONTH 3 English Language & Communication Skills Weeks 9–12
🗣️ Companies test English for 20–30% of placement scores. Good English in interviews also boosts selection chances. Focus: vocabulary, grammar, and communication.
DAILY VOCABULARY ROUTINE
Learn 10 new words/day using Vocabulary.com or Word Power Made Easy — total: 70 words by week end
→ vocabulary.com
For each word, note: meaning, synonym, antonym, example sentence — add to Anki as flashcard
Practice 20 Synonym/Antonym questions daily on IndiaBIX
→ Practice
Read 1 article daily from The Hindu (online, free) — underline unfamiliar words, look up meanings
→ The Hindu
HIGH-FREQUENCY PLACEMENT WORDS
Prioritize these word groups: Adversity/Prosperity, Verbose/Terse, Benevolent/Malevolent, Candid/Evasive, Diligent/Indolent
Root word method: Learn Latin/Greek roots (e.g. "mal" = bad, "bene" = good) — helps decode unknown words in tests
GRAMMAR TOPICS
Tenses (12 types): Focus on Present Perfect, Past Perfect, and Conditionals — most-tested in placements
Subject-Verb Agreement rules — collective nouns, indefinite pronouns, compound subjects
Articles (a/an/the) — vowel sounds rule, proper nouns, superlatives — daily 10-question drill
Prepositions & Conjunctions — "at/on/in" for time and place; subordinating conjunctions (although, unless, since)
Error Detection: Practice 20 sentences/day identifying Part A/B/C/D errors
→ Practice
READING COMPREHENSION STRATEGY
RC Strategy: Read questions FIRST, then skim the passage → saves 2–3 minutes per set
Practice 2 RC passages/day (200–300 words each) with 5 questions each — track your accuracy %
→ Practice
Para Jumbles: Look for topic sentence (often general), connector words (however, therefore, furthermore), and conclusion signal words
Fill in the Blanks: Context-based and grammar-based — 15 questions/day
→ Practice
WRITTEN COMMUNICATION
Practice writing 1 formal email/day — topics: leave application, complaint, request, thank-you (post-interview)
Essay writing (200–300 words): AI in education, Work from home, Climate change, Social media impact — 2 essays/week
Resume language: Use action verbs — "Developed, Implemented, Designed, Collaborated, Optimized" — not "Did" or "Made"
SPEAKING & INTERVIEW ENGLISH
Speak English for 15 minutes/day — record yourself on phone, listen back, identify fillers (um, uh, basically)
GD Topics to prepare: 5G Technology, Remote Work Culture, Data Privacy, Startup vs MNC, AI replacing jobs — write 5 points for and against each
Prepare your "Tell me about yourself" — 90-second script: Background → Skills → Projects → Goals (practice until natural)
End-of-Month English Mock: Take full TCS English section mock (30 questions, 20 minutes)
→ TCS English Mock
MONTHS 4–5 Technical Preparation — CS Core & Coding Weeks 13–20
💻 Technical rounds test your CS fundamentals + coding ability. Start with C/Java, then move to DSA. Aim for 2 LeetCode problems per day from Week 15 onwards.
MUST-KNOW C CONCEPTS
Pointers: Pointer declaration, pointer arithmetic, pointer to arrays, double pointers — these are asked in 80% of C interviews
Memory: Stack vs Heap, malloc/calloc/realloc/free — write 3 programs using each
Strings: strlen, strcpy, strcat, strcmp — implement them manually without library functions
Structures & Unions: Difference between struct and union, size calculation, nested structs
CODING PRACTICE
HackerRank C domain: Complete Easy + Medium challenges — earn 3-star badge
→ Practice
Programs to write: Reverse a string, Check palindrome, Fibonacci, Find duplicates in array, Matrix multiplication
C MCQs on IndiaBIX — 30 questions/day covering output-based and error-finding questions
→ C MCQs
CORE OOP TOPICS (Java preferred for MCA)
4 Pillars: Encapsulation, Abstraction, Inheritance, Polymorphism — explain each with a real-world example + code
Java specifics: Interface vs Abstract class, method overloading vs overriding, static vs instance methods
Exception Handling: try-catch-finally, custom exceptions, checked vs unchecked exceptions
Collections Framework: ArrayList, LinkedList, HashMap, HashSet — when to use which
String class: String vs StringBuilder vs StringBuffer — immutability concept (very common interview Q)
PRACTICE
HackerRank Java domain: Solve 30 problems — focus on OOP, strings, collections
→ Java Practice
IndiaBIX Java MCQs — 25 questions/day (output-tracing questions are gold for interviews)
→ Java MCQs
DATA STRUCTURES TO MASTER
Arrays: Traversal, searching (linear & binary), sorting (bubble, selection, insertion, merge, quick) — know time complexities
Linked List: Singly, doubly, circular — insert, delete, reverse, detect cycle (Floyd's algorithm)
Stack: Using array and linked list — applications: balanced parentheses, infix to postfix, undo operations
Queue: Linear, Circular queue, Deque, Priority Queue — implementation and applications
Hash Table: Hashing, collision resolution (chaining, open addressing) — HashMap implementation
LEETCODE CODING (START HERE)
Start LeetCode Easy problems — target: 2 problems/day. Begin with Array tag then Strings
→ LeetCode Arrays
Must-solve 20 problems: Two Sum, Valid Parentheses, Merge Sorted Array, Reverse String, Palindrome Check, Max Subarray, etc.
GFG DSA Self-Paced course (free) — for theory + implementation side by side
→ GFG DSA
DBMS THEORY (Very High Weightage in MCA Interviews)
ER Diagrams: Entity, Attribute, Relationship — cardinality (1:1, 1:N, M:N) — draw 5 ER diagrams for real-world scenarios
Normalization: 1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF — understand functional dependencies; practice normalizing sample tables
ACID Properties: Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability — explain each with a bank transfer example
Indexes, Keys: Primary, Foreign, Candidate, Super, Unique keys + B-tree indexing concept
Joins: INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, FULL OUTER, SELF JOIN — write examples for each
SQL PRACTICE
SQLZoo or HackerRank SQL: Complete all beginner + intermediate challenges
→ HackerRank SQL
Must-practice queries: GROUP BY + HAVING, subqueries, window functions (ROW_NUMBER, RANK), aggregate functions
Company-asked SQL: "Find 2nd highest salary" (classic!), "Find employees with no manager", "Duplicate rows removal"
OPERATING SYSTEMS (Top Interview Topics)
Process vs Thread: Differences, PCB, thread lifecycle, multi-threading advantages
CPU Scheduling Algorithms: FCFS, SJF, Round Robin, Priority — calculate waiting time + turnaround time for each
Deadlock: 4 Conditions (Mutual Exclusion, Hold & Wait, No Preemption, Circular Wait) + detection, prevention, avoidance (Banker's Algorithm)
Memory Management: Paging, Segmentation, Virtual Memory, Page Replacement (FIFO, LRU, Optimal)
COMPUTER NETWORKS
OSI Model (7 layers): Name, function, and protocols at each layer — memorize mnemonic: "Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away"
TCP vs UDP: Differences, when to use which (HTTP uses TCP; DNS uses UDP)
IP Addressing: IPv4, subnetting, subnet mask, CIDR notation — practice 10 subnetting calculations
HTTP/HTTPS, DNS, DHCP, FTP, SSH — how each protocol works (conceptual, not implementation)
SOFTWARE ENGINEERING
SDLC Models: Waterfall, Agile, Spiral, V-model — when to use which; Agile/Scrum is most relevant today
Design Patterns (know conceptually): Singleton, Factory, Observer, MVC — explain each in plain English
Testing types: Unit, Integration, System, UAT, Black-box vs White-box testing
PYTHON (Bonus — High Demand)
Python basics in 5 days: Lists, Tuples, Dicts, Sets; List comprehension; Lambda functions; File I/O
→ HackerRank Python
OOP in Python: Classes, inheritance, dunder methods — write a mini project (Student Management System)
GFG Python course (free) — complete in 1 week parallel to other topics
→ GFG Python

🏁 This is your sprint month. Switch from learning mode to exam mode. Simulate real placement conditions. Fix your resume. Practise HR rounds daily.

→ Infosys Mock

→ Wipro Mock

MUST-PREPARE TECHNICAL QUESTIONS
OOP Questions (10): What is polymorphism? Difference between interface and abstract class? What is method hiding vs overriding? etc.
DBMS Questions (10): What are ACID properties? Difference between DELETE and TRUNCATE? What is a view? Triggers vs Stored Procedures?
OS Questions (10): What is a deadlock? Difference between process and thread? What is virtual memory? Explain paging.
CN Questions (10): What is the OSI model? Difference between TCP and UDP? What happens when you type google.com?
DSA Questions (10): Explain sorting algorithms and time complexities. What is a binary search tree? Reverse a linked list (code it live).
CODING ROUND PREP
LeetCode Top Interview 150 list — solve 5 problems/day; focus on Easy and then Medium
→ Top 150
Practice explaining your approach out loud before writing code — interviewers care about your thinking process
HR INTERVIEW QUESTIONS TO PREPARE
"Tell me about yourself" — 90-second structured answer: Name → MCA background → Skills → Projects → Career goal
"Why this company?" — Research the company before every interview: products, values, recent news, competitors
"Strengths & Weaknesses" — Be specific; for weakness, always follow up with how you're improving it
"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" — Align with the company's growth; show ambition but realistic goals
Situational questions (STAR method): Situation, Task, Action, Result — prepare 5 stories from college projects/internships
RESUME CHECKLIST
1-page resume only — use Novoresume or Overleaf (Jake's template) for clean, ATS-friendly format
→ Novoresume
Must have sections: Contact Info → Summary (2 lines) → Education → Skills → Projects (2-3) → Certifications → Achievements
Projects: Add GitHub link and a 2-line description of what problem it solves + tech stack used
Skills section: Languages → Frameworks → Tools → Databases — be honest, only list what you can speak about confidently
Proofread 3 times — zero spelling/grammar errors; have a friend or professor review it
FINAL REVISION PLAN
Monday — Aptitude Blitz: Solve 60 mixed aptitude questions (all topics) in 45 minutes. Review Anki formula cards.
Tuesday — Technical Rapid Fire: Answer 50 technical MCQs (CS fundamentals) in 30 minutes
→ GFG Quiz
Wednesday — Mock GD: Join a friend/study group; pick a topic (e.g., "AI is a threat to jobs"), discuss 15 minutes — record and evaluate
Thursday — Full Mock Interview: Ask a friend to interview you — 30 min technical + 15 min HR. Use Zoom/phone for realism.
Friday — English Polish: Review vocabulary Anki deck. Practice "Tell me about yourself" once more. Read 1 RC passage.
Weekend — Rest + Confidence: Light review only. Sleep 8 hours. Organize documents (certificates, resume copies, ID proofs).
